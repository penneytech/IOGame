Power Manual

New to all this? Start with the step-by-step Lessons — six short pages that walk you through building your first character.

Your character lives in character.py. You write a function build_character() that returns a dictionary. Inside powers is a list of power dicts, and every power has the same outer shape:

{
  "name": "My Power",
  "key": "space",          # press this key to use it
  "cooldownMs": 800,       # 150 .. 15000
  "cast": { "kind": "...", ...stuff that depends on the kind }
}

Python tip: use True / False (capital first letter) and None instead of JS true / false / null.

The 6 cast kinds

The cast.kind picks how the power is delivered.

1. projectile

Fires one or more flying objects from your character.

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hexColour of the projectile.
speed50 .. 900Pixels per second.
radius2 .. 30Bigger = easier to hit with.
lifetimeMs200 .. 5000How long it lives before fading.
count1 .. 6Number of projectiles fired at once.
spreadDeg0 .. 180How wide the fan of projectiles is (only matters when count > 1).
piercetrue / falseIf true, the projectile passes through one target and keeps going.
onHit1 .. 3 effectsWhat happens to whoever it hits.

2. area

Drops a circle on the ground that ticks effects to anyone (other than you) standing in it.

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hexColour of the area.
radius20 .. 150Size of the circle.
durationMs100 .. 6000How long it lasts.
tickIntervalMs100 .. 1000How often it applies its effects.
onTick1 .. 3 effectsEffects applied each tick.

3. melee

An instant cone-shaped swing in front of you. No projectile travels.

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hexVisual flash colour.
range20 .. 140How far in front of you the swing reaches.
arcDeg30 .. 180How wide the cone is.
onHit1 .. 3 effectsEffects applied to anyone caught in the cone.

4. dash

Quickly move yourself in your facing direction. Great for escaping.

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hex(Currently cosmetic.)
distance40 .. 400How far you teleport.
durationMs80 .. 600If invulnerable, you're untouchable for this long.
invulnerabletrue / falseAvoid all damage during the dash.

5. shield

Self-buff. Absorbs incoming damage up to amount.

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hexAura colour.
amount5 .. 200HP of damage soaked.
durationMs200 .. 8000How long it lasts.

6. heal

Self-buff. Instantly restore HP (capped at your max).

FieldRangeWhat it does
colorname or #hexVisual colour.
amount1 .. 80HP restored.

The 6 effect kinds

Effects go inside onHit (projectile / melee) or onTick (area).

EffectFieldsWhat it does
damage amount 1..60 Reduces target HP. Blocked by an active shield.
heal amount 1..80 Increases target HP (rare on offensive powers, but allowed in areas).
slow factor 0.1..0.95
durationMs 100..6000		 Multiplies the target's movement speed for the duration.
stun durationMs 100..2000 Target can't move or fire.
knockback strength 50..600 Pushes the target away from the source of the hit.
dot dps 1..30
durationMs 100..6000		 Damage over time. Multiple DoTs stack.

A complete build_character()

Drop powers into powers and return the whole character:

def build_character():
    fireball = {
        "name": "Fireball", "key": "space", "cooldownMs": 600,
        "cast": {
            "kind": "projectile", "color": "orange",
            "speed": 540, "radius": 6, "lifetimeMs": 1400,
            "count": 1,
            "onHit": [
                {"effect": "damage", "amount": 16},
                {"effect": "dot", "dps": 8, "durationMs": 1500},
            ],
        },
    }

    blink = {
        "name": "Blink", "key": "f", "cooldownMs": 3500,
        "cast": {
            "kind": "dash", "color": "white",
            "distance": 220, "durationMs": 200, "invulnerable": True,
        },
    }

    return {
        "characterName": "My Mage",
        "color": "#ff6a3d",
        "size": 22,
        "speed": 230,
        "maxHealth": 90,
        "powers": [fireball, blink],
    }

The first power’s key is the one that fires when you click; the rest fire when you press their key. Use single-character keys ("space", "q", "e", "f") so you can reach them while moving with WASD.

Controls & movement

  • WASD or arrow keys to move. Movement has a little weight — quick taps now feel like dodges.
  • Mouse aims your character. You can run one way while shooting another, just like a real twin-stick game.
  • Shift to sprint, C to dodge-roll (brief invulnerability), M to mute sound.
  • Each power has its own key (set in your manifest). Press it once to fire — the cooldown is shown above your hotbar.

Game modes

  • Solo (FFA) — every player for themself. Score = kills − deaths.
  • Team match — players are split into Blue and Red. Friendly fire is OFF, so you can support teammates with heals and shields safely. Whichever team has the higher total score at the end wins.
  • Capture the Flag — two flags, one base each. Touch the enemy flag to pick it up, then carry it back to your own base to score. Die while carrying and the flag returns home. First team to 3 captures wins.

The teacher chooses the mode when starting each round.

Build budget & balance

Every character has a hard cap of 100 build points. The server will reject any manifest that costs more. The editor shows you a live cost breakdown after Run & Validate.

Roughly speaking:

  • Stat costs — extra HP, extra speed, smaller hitbox, and extra power slots all cost points. The first power is "free".
  • Power costs = cast cost × cooldown factor. A short cooldown multiplies a power's cost; a long cooldown shrinks it.
  • Effect costs — damage, heal, slow, stun, knockback, and DoT each cost based on their numbers.

So you really cannot make a character that has max HP, max speed, max damage, and instant cooldowns. The system forces tradeoffs.

How effects interact (the hierarchy)

  • Shield first, then HP. Damage is absorbed by an active shield until it breaks; only the leftover comes off HP.
  • Heal stacks with shield — they don't interact. Healing only refills HP, never shield.
  • DoTs from the same source refresh, they don't stack. If you tag someone with a 6dps/2s DoT and re-tag them 0.5s later, you don't get 12 dps — you keep 6 dps and the timer resets. DoTs from different attackers stack normally.
  • Slow takes the strongest active. Two overlapping slows: the lower factor wins (more slow), and the longer duration wins. Slow doesn't stack to 0.
  • Stun overrides movement entirely. A stunned target can't move or cast. Multiple stuns just refresh to the longest.
  • Knockback is a velocity impulse. It pushes you, then friction slows you back down. Bigger strength = farther push.
  • Dash invulnerability beats everything for its duration — even DoT ticks land on 0.

Fun-but-fair philosophy

The goal is not to make the most overpowered character. The goal is to design something creative, readable, and fun for someone else to play against. A character that wins by being annoying loses respect. Aim for something you'd enjoy fighting.
